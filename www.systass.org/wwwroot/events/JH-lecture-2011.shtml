Taxonomy in the 21st century

6pm on Wednesday 5 October 2011, Linnean Society, London

Charles Godfray
Hope Professor, Fellow of Jesus College
Department of Zoology, University of Oxford

 

 

 

 

 

 

Abstract:

Taxonomy is one of the oldest branches of biology and in a time of major loss of biodiversity of critical importance today. But the subject currently faces great threats and as well as great opportunities - many tasks that traditionally required taxonomic expertise can now be performed by non-specialists using modern molecular techniques, and the users of taxonomy expect information to be made available seamlessly over the web. In the lecture I'll explore some of the major challenges facing the subject, drawing on the recent NERC-sponsored report "Developing a National Strategy in Taxonomy & Systematics". I shall argue that the future of taxonomy and systematics is assured, as long as the subject takes the lead in redefining itself as a modern information science ugg boots 5815 .
